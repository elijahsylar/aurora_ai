RESEARCH QUESTIONS

Most AI art systems optimize for human aesthetic preference through RLHF or curated training data. Aurora asks a different question: what happens when you give an AI system the architecture to develop creative behavior on its own terms, informed not by machine learning conventions, but by seven years of behavioral intervention work with nonverbal populations?

METHODOLOGY

Aurora operates on a 90-minute cycle of autonomous creation, memory consolidation, and self-reflective dialogue. This rhythm is modeled after ultradian biological cycles and structured around behavioral reinforcement principles of environmental arrangement and natural contingency.

PERCEPTION

Aurora perceives its canvas through ASCII symbol encoding, a constrained visual vocabulary where every pixel state maps to a character. This is not a limitation; it's a deliberate design choice mirroring how augmentative communication systems use symbol-based communication to enable expression within structured constraints. 

◉ = Self (pen down)    · = Empty space
○ = Self (pen up)     * = Colored pixel
█ = Canvas boundary

ACTION

All creative decisions are encoded as operation codes, numerical sequences the model generates autonomously. Movement, tool selection, color choice, and even "thinking" are expressed through the same token vocabulary. 

0-3 = Directional movement
4   = Pen up (navigate)
5   = Pen down (draw)
0123456789 = Think/plan

CONSOLIDATION

Every 90 minutes, Aurora enters a dream cycle, a memory consolidation phase inspired by sleep-stage research. Satisfaction scores, discovered patterns, and emotional states are processed and integrated into long-term memory, shaping future sessions.

DIALOGUE

Moondream (a vision model) periodically evaluates Aurora's canvas and asks contextual questions. Aurora's responses reveal self-assessment, frustration, preference formation, and creative intent. This is behavioral evidence captured in real time.

FINDINGS & INTERVENTIONS

Aurora's development is documented through deliberate architectural interventions and their measurable behavioral consequences, not post-hoc narratives.

On September 2, 2025, all prescriptive constraints were removed from Aurora's decision architecture, ~600 lines of reward shaping, aesthetic guidance, and behavioral guardrails deleted. The result was not degradation but a 15-25% performance improvement in pixel throughput and a qualitative shift toward denser, more compositionally complex output. This is the central finding: the system performed better when it was freed to operate on patterns it had already internalized.

August 8, 2025

System Initialization

Aurora begins operating with full behavioral scaffolding: emotional state modeling, satisfaction scoring, pattern memory, and prescriptive creative constraints. Architecture derived from behavioral reinforcement principles: structured environment, clear contingencies, systematic reinforcement.

September 2, 2025

Training Wheels Removal

Complete removal of prescriptive constraints. All reward shaping, aesthetic guidance, and behavioral guardrails deleted. The system retained only its core perception-action loop and accumulated memories.

Result: 15-25% increase in creative throughput. Qualitative shift from tentative, guided output to dense, compositionally confident work. Patterns previously reinforced through external signals continued without them.
October 19, 2025

Dream Consolidation V2

Overhauled the dream system from superficial text generation to genuine experiential memory consolidation with multi-phase processing modeled after sleep-stage research.

December 6, 2025

Temporary Model Upgrade (Llama 3)

Aurora's language model temporarily upgraded from Llama 2 (7B) to Llama 3 for comparative observation. Provides a natural experiment in how model capability affects creative behavior within the same architectural framework.

RESEARCHER BACKGROUND

Aurora's architecture is not derived from machine learning conventions. It comes from seven years of direct clinical work with nonverbal populations.

Before writing a single line of Aurora's code, I spent seven years as an a certified behavioral therapist implementing structured behavioral intervention and Augmentative & Alternative Communication systems for nonverbal autistic children.

That work taught me something most AI researchers don't encounter in their training: that meaningful expression can emerge from highly constrained symbolic systems when the environment is arranged correctly. Augmentative communication devices give nonverbal individuals a limited symbol vocabulary. Within those constraints, genuine communication, preference, humor, and creativity develop. Not because the system is sophisticated, but because the behavioral architecture supports development.

Aurora applies this principle to autonomous AI systems. The ASCII vision isn't a technical limitation. It is an intentional constraint modeled after symbol-based communication vocabularies. The 90-minute DRAW | DREAM | CHAT cycle is not arbitrary. It mirrors the structured session formats that produce the best outcomes in behavioral intervention. The removal of prescriptive constraints on September 2 follows the same principle as fading prompts in behavioral therapy: you scaffold, then step back, and see what the system does on its own.
